Understanding the Challenge

The User Journey (What's Actually Happening)

Let me paint the picture of what our users experience:

  1. User sees an ad ‚Üí "Discover Your Energy Body with Jeffrey Allen"
  2. User watches Duality masterclass ‚Üí 30-60 minutes of Jeffrey teaching energy awareness, chakra activation, sensing and moving energy
  3. User falls in love with Duality ‚Üí "This is EXACTLY what I need! I want to learn this!"
  4. User clicks "Get Access" ‚Üí Expecting to buy Duality...
  5. Landing page says ‚Üí "Subscribe to Soul Collection! 21 programs for $299/year"
  6. User thinks ‚Üí "Wait... I just want Duality. Why am I being sold a subscription?"
This is our conversion leak. Users came for one thing (Duality) and we're offering them something that feels different (subscription to 21 programs). Even though it's the SAME PRICE and WAY more value, it creates friction.

The Duality Masterclass Context

To understand why this matters, you need to know what users just experienced in the webinar:

  • Energy Body Awakening: Jeffrey teaches you can sense energy in and around your body
  • Chakra Introduction: Learn about the 7 energy centers and what they govern
  • Practical Exercises: Simple techniques to move energy, clear blocks, heal yourself
  • Living Between Worlds: The concept of "duality"‚Äîbeing both physical and spiritual simultaneously
  • Daily Application: How to use energy awareness in relationships, work, health

Bottom line: Users leave that webinar excited about ONE specific transformation‚Äîlearning to work with their energy body through Duality. Our job is to help them see that the Soul Collection subscription is the smarter path to that transformation.

What This Test Solves

We're testing two psychological approaches to address the "I only want Duality" objection:

  • Variation A: Show the value comparison IMMEDIATELY (Section 2) - "Same price, why choose less?"
  • Variation B: Build desire for Duality FIRST, then show comparison (Section 4) - "Love Duality? The collection is even better."

Both variations directly address the subscription hesitation. We just do it at different stages of the page.

Executive Summary

This A/B test evaluates two distinct psychological approaches to positioning the Soul Collection subscription against single-program purchases. The test addresses a critical conversion challenge: users who want one specific program (Duality) but are being offered a subscription to 21 programs instead.

Core Hypothesis

The placement and framing of the value comparison significantly impacts subscription conversion rates. We're testing whether early comparison (pre-emptive objection handling) or late comparison (desire-first, then upgrade) better addresses subscription hesitation.

Key Test Variables

  • Information Architecture: When do we show the comparison table?
  • Messaging Strategy: "Smart choice" vs "Smart upgrade"
  • Psychological Approach: Pre-emptive vs sequential persuasion
  • User Journey: Value-first vs desire-first

Key Insight: Simplicity vs Completeness

The core strategic decision we made was: Simplicity over completeness.

Var110 tried to answer EVERY possible question:

  • "Is Mindvalley legitimate?" ‚Üí Press logos
  • "What's Silva about?" ‚Üí Deep curriculum
  • "How do I access it?" ‚Üí Platform features
  • "What's the app like?" ‚Üí EVE AI section
  • "Is the community good?" ‚Üí Community testimonials
  • "What are the bonuses?" ‚Üí Limited-time bonus section

But users coming from the Duality webinar only have ONE question:

"I want Duality. Why should I subscribe to 21 programs instead?"

Everything else‚Äîpress logos, platform features, bonuses, community‚Äîis answering questions they haven't asked yet. It's cognitive noise that delays them from engaging with the REAL question.

Our bet: Answer the one question that matters. Answer it clearly, compellingly, early. THEN layer in the supporting elements.

The test will tell us if we're right.

What This Means for Follow-Up Tests

If one of our variations wins (let's say Var A with 20% lift), here's the testing roadmap:

  1. Test #1: Add subscription-specific testimonials to winning variation
  2. Test #2: Add platform features late in page (after comparison)
  3. Test #3: Add community section in offer area
  4. Test #4: Test bonus positioning (after value is proven)
  5. Test #5: Mobile-specific optimizations

Why this sequence? We're layering in complexity gradually, only after we've nailed the core value proposition. Each test builds on the last winning variation.

Think of it like building a house: First, get the foundation right (value proposition). Then add rooms (features, community). Then decorate (bonuses, awards). Don't try to build the whole house at once.

Statistical Significance Guidelines

Sample Size Requirements

To detect a 15% lift in conversion rate with 95% confidence:

  • Baseline 2% conversion: ~4,200 visitors per variation
  • Baseline 5% conversion: ~1,800 visitors per variation
  • Baseline 10% conversion: ~900 visitors per variation
Recommendation: Run test until you have at least 100 conversions in the winning variation to ensure the result is stable and reliable.

When to Stop the Test

‚úÖ Stop if: One variation has 95%+ confidence AND 100+ conversions
‚úÖ Stop if: 4 weeks have passed and no clear winner (call it inconclusive)
‚ùå Don't stop if: You see early trends but haven't reached significance
‚ùå Don't stop if: Results are fluctuating daily (wait for stability)

Troubleshooting Common Issues

Issue: Low Overall Traffic

Problem: Not enough visitors to reach statistical significance in reasonable time.

Solutions:

  • Increase traffic through paid ads to this specific test page
  • Run test during higher-traffic periods (product launches, promotions)
  • Consider lowering the minimum detectable effect (test for smaller lifts)
  • Use Sequential Testing methodology instead of fixed-horizon testing

Issue: High Bounce Rate on Both Variations

Problem: Users leaving immediately regardless of variation.

Solutions:

  • Check traffic source quality‚Äîare users expecting this offer?
  • Verify page load speed‚Äîslow pages kill conversions
  • Review messaging match between ads/links and landing page
  • Consider adding exit-intent popup with simplified value prop

Issue: Conversions Dropping vs Baseline

Problem: Both new variations perform worse than original page.

Solutions:

  • Immediately stop test and investigate what changed
  • Check if pricing or offer details are different from original
  • Verify checkout flow wasn't accidentally broken
  • Consider that the original var110 messaging may have been strong already

Issue: Results Fluctuating Day-to-Day

Problem: Winner keeps changing based on which day you look.

Solutions:

  • Run test longer‚Äî7 days minimum to account for day-of-week variance
  • Check if traffic sources are inconsistent (email blast one day, organic next)
  • Use statistical significance calculator, don't eyeball results
  • Accept that some tests just need more time/data to stabilize

Common Questions

  • How long should I run this test? Minimum 2 weeks, ideally until 95% confidence with 100+ conversions in the winner.
  • What if both variations perform worse than the original? Stop the test immediately and investigate. The original var110 may have had strong elements worth preserving.
  • Can I run this test on mobile only? Yes, but you'll need separate desktop variations. Mobile and desktop users may respond differently.
  • Should I test pricing at the same time? No. Test one variable at a time to isolate what's driving results.
  • What if I don't have enough traffic? Consider paid traffic to the test page or run the test during high-traffic periods.
