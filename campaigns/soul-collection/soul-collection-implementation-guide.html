Executive Summary

This A/B test evaluates two distinct psychological approaches to positioning the Soul Collection subscription against single-program purchases. The test addresses a critical conversion challenge: users who want one specific program (Duality) but are being offered a subscription to 21 programs instead.

Core Hypothesis

The placement and framing of the value comparison significantly impacts subscription conversion rates. We're testing whether early comparison (pre-emptive objection handling) or late comparison (desire-first, then upgrade) better addresses subscription hesitation.

Key Test Variables

  • Information Architecture: When do we show the comparison table?
  • Messaging Strategy: "Smart choice" vs "Smart upgrade"
  • Psychological Approach: Pre-emptive vs sequential persuasion
  • User Journey: Value-first vs desire-first

The Two Variations Explained

Variation A

Subscription as Smart Choice

Strategy: Early Comparison

Psychological Approach: Address the "but I only want one program" objection immediately by showing the value comparison upfront.

Section Flow:

1 Hero: "Get Duality + 20 Programs for the Same Price"
2 Comparison Table: Immediate value comparison (pre-emptive)
3 Duality Spotlight: What you came for is included
4 20 Programs Grid: What else you get
5 Meditations: Plus 250+ meditations
6 Offer: Final conversion

Best For:

  • Analytical decision-makers
  • Value-conscious buyers
  • Users with price objections
  • Comparison shoppers
Variation B

Subscription as Upgrade

Strategy: Late Comparison

Psychological Approach: Build desire for Duality first, then show why the full collection is the smarter upgrade.

Section Flow:

1 Hero: "You Discovered Duality. Now Upgrade."
2 Duality Spotlight: Build desire for what you want
3 Journey Enhancement: Why one program isn't enough
4 Comparison Table: "And here's why collection is better"
5 20 Programs Grid: Your complete toolkit
6 Meditations: Plus 250+ meditations
7 Offer: Final conversion

Best For:

  • Emotionally-driven buyers
  • Committed spiritual seekers
  • Users who trust teachers
  • Growth-oriented mindset

Statistical Significance Guidelines

Sample Size Requirements

To detect a 15% lift in conversion rate with 95% confidence:

  • Baseline 2% conversion: ~4,200 visitors per variation
  • Baseline 5% conversion: ~1,800 visitors per variation
  • Baseline 10% conversion: ~900 visitors per variation
Recommendation: Run test until you have at least 100 conversions in the winning variation to ensure the result is stable and reliable.

When to Stop the Test

‚úÖ Stop if: One variation has 95%+ confidence AND 100+ conversions
‚úÖ Stop if: 4 weeks have passed and no clear winner (call it inconclusive)
‚ùå Don't stop if: You see early trends but haven't reached significance
‚ùå Don't stop if: Results are fluctuating daily (wait for stability)

Interpreting Results

Scenario What It Means Recommended Action
Clear Winner (15%+ lift) One approach significantly outperforms Implement winning variation immediately
Marginal Winner (5-10% lift) Small but meaningful improvement Implement winner, continue optimizing
No Significant Difference Both variations perform similarly Choose based on secondary metrics or user experience
Inconclusive (high variance) Results are too unstable to trust Run longer or redesign test

Troubleshooting Common Issues

Issue: Low Overall Traffic

Problem: Not enough visitors to reach statistical significance in reasonable time.

Solutions:

  • Increase traffic through paid ads to this specific test page
  • Run test during higher-traffic periods (product launches, promotions)
  • Consider lowering the minimum detectable effect (test for smaller lifts)
  • Use Sequential Testing methodology instead of fixed-horizon testing

Issue: High Bounce Rate on Both Variations

Problem: Users leaving immediately regardless of variation.

Solutions:

  • Check traffic source quality‚Äîare users expecting this offer?
  • Verify page load speed‚Äîslow pages kill conversions
  • Review messaging match between ads/links and landing page
  • Consider adding exit-intent popup with simplified value prop

Issue: Conversions Dropping vs Baseline

Problem: Both new variations perform worse than original page.

Solutions:

  • Immediately stop test and investigate what changed
  • Check if pricing or offer details are different from original
  • Verify checkout flow wasn't accidentally broken
  • Consider that the original var110 messaging may have been strong already

Issue: Results Fluctuating Day-to-Day

Problem: Winner keeps changing based on which day you look.

Solutions:

  • Run test longer‚Äî7 days minimum to account for day-of-week variance
  • Check if traffic sources are inconsistent (email blast one day, organic next)
  • Use statistical significance calculator, don't eyeball results
  • Accept that some tests just need more time/data to stabilize

Common Questions

  • How long should I run this test? Minimum 2 weeks, ideally until 95% confidence with 100+ conversions in the winner.
  • What if both variations perform worse than the original? Stop the test immediately and investigate. The original var110 may have had strong elements worth preserving.
  • Can I run this test on mobile only? Yes, but you'll need separate desktop variations. Mobile and desktop users may respond differently.
  • Should I test pricing at the same time? No. Test one variable at a time to isolate what's driving results.
  • What if I don't have enough traffic? Consider paid traffic to the test page or run the test during high-traffic periods.
