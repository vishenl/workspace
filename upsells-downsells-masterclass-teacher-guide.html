Slide 4: The Truth About Buying (6-12 min)

üé§ Script

"Here's the thing most marketers get wrong: They think upselling is pushy. They think customers will feel manipulated. But the data tells a completely different story."

"When someone just purchased from you, they're in what I call 'maximum receptivity.' They've crossed the psychological threshold. They trust you. They're committed to transformation. And‚Äîthis is key‚Äîthey're actually LOOKING for ways to accelerate their results."

[Point to the two columns on screen]

"This is what's happening in their mind vs. what they're open to. Do you see the alignment? They WANT more. Your job isn't to convince them‚Äîit's to SERVE them by offering the right next step."

Teaching Note: Address Ethical Concerns HEAD-ON

This is where you defuse the #1 objection students have: "Isn't this manipulative?" Frame it as SERVICE, not extraction. Use phrases like "meeting them where they are" and "helping them succeed faster." This reframe is crucial for buy-in.

üí° Quick Reflection (Optional, if time allows)

Slide 5: Daniel Priestley's Value Ladder (12-18 min)

üé§ Script

"Now I want to introduce you to one of the most powerful frameworks in modern marketing. It comes from Daniel Priestley, author of 'Key Person of Influence' and one of the smartest people I know in business strategy."

[Read the quote aloud]

"People don't buy products. They buy outcomes. And they'll pay more for certainty.' Think about that. Your customers aren't asking 'what's the cheapest option?'‚Äîthey're asking 'what's going to WORK?'"

"This is the Value Ladder. It's simple: You create ascending tiers of offers, each delivering MORE value and MORE certainty. Low-ticket gets them started. Mid-ticket gets them results. High-ticket gets them transformation with support."

Teaching Note: Use Analogies

If students look confused, use an analogy: "Think about a gym membership. Basic tier: $20/month, access to equipment. Mid-tier: $100/month, includes group classes. Premium: $300/month, personal trainer. Same outcome (fitness), different levels of support and certainty."

‚ö†Ô∏è Don't Rush This

The Value Ladder is FOUNDATIONAL. Everything else builds on it. Students must understand this concept before you move to tactics. Spend the full 6 minutes here.

Slide 6: The 3 Types of Upsells (18-25 min)

üé§ Script

"Okay, now that you understand the ladder, let's get tactical. There are three types of upsells, and you need to know when to use each one."

[Walk through each type]

"The 'Better' upsell: You upgrade what they just bought. Course becomes course + coaching calls. Basic becomes premium."

"The 'Faster' upsell: You add something that accelerates results. Templates, done-for-you services, 1-on-1 support."

"The 'More' upsell: You offer complementary products. They bought a fitness program? Offer a nutrition program. They're related but not the same."

"All three work. The question isn't which is 'best'‚Äîit's which matches customer intent in that specific moment."

üí° Breakout Activity (5 min)

If you have 30+ students, do a quick 3-minute breakout: "With your partner, identify which type of upsell would work best for YOUR business. Share examples."

Then bring everyone back and have 2-3 people share their ideas.
