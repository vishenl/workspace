‚ö†Ô∏è The 10 Biggest Student Struggles

These friction points appear across ALL programs. Avoid them to dramatically improve student experience.

1. Tech & Access Issues

#1 pain point. Links, logins, replays, scattered resources cause major frustration.

2. Overwhelm & Content Density

Too much content, sessions too close together, too many frameworks too fast.

3. Pacing & Long Calls

Sessions feel long, dense, or fast. Need tighter segments and clearer transitions.

4. Clarity Gaps & Navigation

Students struggle with what to focus on now, where to find resources, how topics connect.

5. Too Advanced for Beginners

Appears in every Mastery. Students want clearer on-ramps.

6. Repetition / Overlap

When ideas repeat across sessions, students want explicit layering.

7. Missing or Delayed Resources

Even small delays break momentum and lower perceived professionalism.

8. Not Enough Demos or Examples

Especially in advanced or conceptual lessons.

9. Unequal Q&A Participation

Students want fair rotation and more coaching opportunities.

10. Time Zones & Scheduling

Global audiences struggle with long calls live.

Essential principles for structuring your Mastery experience

Core Principles

üë§ One Main Teacher

Have one main teacher deliver the experience. This provides consistency and greatly assists student experience and implementation.

üì¶ Pre-Session Material Delivery

Authors must send all materials and links BEFORE the class. This should be a contractual requirement.

  • Tools, slides, links, books, apps
  • Any reading or downloads required
  • Special preparation instructions

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical: Especially important for technical Masteries like AI. Communicate preparation requirements to students by email beforehand.

üéØ Maximum 3 Main Learning Points per Class

Every class should have a maximum of 3 main learning points so people have clear outcomes.

  • Map these at the beginning to mentally prepare students
  • This directs the experience and creates a nurturing environment
  • When you map out the lesson, students can relax into the experience

üîß Actionable Tools (1-2 per Class)

Every class must include practical, immediately usable tools:

  • Frameworks
  • Checklists
  • Templates
  • Scripts
  • Exercises

Q&A Best Practices

End every session with an open Q&A segment following these guidelines:

‚úÖ Do This

  • Direct all questions to Zoom Q&A box (not chat‚Äîit creates distraction)
  • Ask students to upvote questions
  • Address most relevant questions for the whole community
  • Track who asks questions across all classes
  • Ensure fair rotation‚Äîgive more people chances

‚ùå Avoid This

  • Answering questions from chat
  • Allowing same students to dominate every session
  • Skipping Q&A due to time overruns
  • Answering questions not relevant to broader group

Time Management

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical: Be Mindful of Time

People complain when calls go over time because they get FOMO if they "miss anything."

  • Even though replays exist, it's not the same
  • Most students have other commitments on their calendar
  • Respect the scheduled end time
  • Build buffer time into your segments

üåü Best Practices & Pro Tips

Structure Every Session

Use clear segments: Teaching ‚Üí Tool ‚Üí Demo ‚Üí Q&A. Students need to know where they are in the journey.

Provide One-Pagers

Create summary sheets with key steps, frameworks, and prompts for every major session.

Show, Don't Just Tell

Live demos, before/after examples, and case studies accelerate understanding dramatically.

Layer Content Intentionally

When revisiting topics, explicitly frame as "Advanced layer on X" so it feels like progression.

Create Clear Pathways

Offer beginner and advanced tracks. Label sessions by level so students know what to prioritize.

End with Next Steps

Every session should close with: "Here's exactly what you do for the next 7 days."

Centralize Resources

All links, slides, tools, replays in ONE clearly-labeled hub. No hunting across channels.

Build Breathing Room

Include integration weeks after major clusters. Focus on guided practice and Q&A‚Äîno new frameworks.

Share Your Journey

Students trust practitioners. Share your real experiences, struggles, and transformations.

Support Emotional Safety

Show vulnerability and authenticity. Students go deeper when they feel safe and seen.

Facilitate Community

Use breakouts, shares, and pods. Growing together reduces isolation and increases engagement.

Respect Time Boundaries

Never go overtime. Build buffer into your segments. Students have commitments.
