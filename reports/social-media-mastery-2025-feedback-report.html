7. Actionable Recommendations

üéØIMMEDIATE PRIORITIES:

1. Restructure Course Order:

  • Week 1-2: Basics (equipment, platforms, branding, accounts)
  • Week 3-4: Confidence building (Prince EA, Sarah Snow)
  • Week 5-8: Viral Content Model & Formats
  • Week 9-12: Advanced strategy with Vishen
  • Final weeks: Implementation & review

2. More Vishen Sessions:

  • Students explicitly request more Vishen throughout
  • His sessions consistently rated 9.5-10/10
  • Tech integration and strategic content is missing

3. Fix Breakout Rooms:

  • Clear timing structure: 5 min per person max
  • Assign facilitators or rotate leadership
  • Group by experience level and industry
  • Provide written instructions before breakout

4. Better Q&A Management:

  • "Token system" - each student gets limited live questions
  • Time limits: 5 minutes per student max
  • Save Q&A for END of content delivery
  • Rotate who gets called - track participation

5. Technical Resources Hub:

  • Create ONE consolidated sheet with all AI tools, apps, equipment
  • Add edited tutorial videos for each tool (CapCut, Vocal, etc.)
  • Step-by-step guides for camera/lighting setup
  • Better Viral Trends onboarding

6. Content Variety:

  • Break up "Andrew content review" sameness
  • Alternate between: strategy, technical, mindset, AI tools, live reviews
  • More industry-specific examples (not just coaches/wellness)
  • Format-specific deep dives for different niches

7. Access & Inclusion:

  • Extend access period or make evergreen (most requested!)
  • Add captions to all recordings
  • Earlier access to key info (equipment lists, format guides)
  • Fair review selection - track who's been reviewed

8. Jeff King's Sessions:

  • Pre-record content, then do live Q&A separately
  • OR: Strict structure with Q&A only at designated breaks
  • Less banter/joking, more focused content delivery

9. Final Insights

The Core Tension:

This course suffers from trying to be both comprehensive AND fast-paced, resulting in:

  • Information overload for beginners
  • Too slow/repetitive for advanced students
  • Key information coming too late
  • Format confusion persisting for months

The Secret Sauce:

Ileana's facilitation is what holds this course together. Every speaker benefits from her support, and she's mentioned more positively than any instructor except Vishen. Invest in training more facilitators like her.

The Bottom Line:

Students LOVE the potential of this course but want:

  1. Better structure and pacing
  2. More Vishen and variety
  3. Less repetitive content review
  4. Earlier access to basics
  5. Longer/evergreen access
  6. More consolidated resources
  7. Fairer participation opportunities
