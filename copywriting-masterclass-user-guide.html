‚ö° The 5 Golden Rules of Copywriting

Rule #1: Write for No More Than 15-Year-Old Comprehension Level

Why it matters: Multiple studies prove that content matching a 15-year-old's reading level converts better across ALL audiences, regardless of education level.

How to Test:

  1. Go to HemingwayApp.com
  2. Copy your website text
  3. Paste it into Hemingway
  4. Check your grade level (aim for grade 5-8)
‚úÖ Example from Class:

When Henri tested her website, it came back as Grade 5 (approximately 10-11 years old) - Perfect! This proves that even complex transformation work can be explained simply.

Rule #2: Have a Clear Call to Action (CTA)

The Problem: Most websites confuse visitors with multiple competing CTAs.

‚ùå Common Mistake:

"Let's Book Clarity" + "Empower" + "Impact" + "Free Guide" + "Reignite Your Flame" + "My Story" + "Let's Connect"

Result: Visitor confusion = No action taken

‚úÖ Solution:

ONE primary call to action throughout the page:

"Download Your Free Guide"

(appears 2-3 times)

Everything else is secondary or removed.

Rule #3: It's Not About Making a Sale - It's About Getting Users to USE the Product

The Mindvalley Case Study:

Initial Approach:

"Download your free audio and ebook" ‚Üí Pop-up ‚Üí Instant download

Result: 20% MORE emails, but 30% FEWER sales üìâ

Why?

People weren't reading the ebook because they didn't understand why they needed it.

Solution:

Create a dedicated landing page explaining:

  • What's in the guide
  • Why you need it
  • Benefits you'll receive
  • Social proof
  • How to use it

Apply This To:

  • Free guides: Create a landing page that sells the guide
  • Strategy calls: Explain what happens on the call
  • Products: Create a "Welcome" page showing how to use the product

Rule #4: All Buttons Must Have the Same Messaging

Small changes in button text can significantly impact conversions.

Context ‚ùå Avoid ‚úÖ Use This
First purchase "Add to Cart" "Add to Cart"
Upsell (after purchase) "Add to Cart" "Add to My Order"
Free guide "Click Here" ‚Üí "Get Guide" ‚Üí "Download" "Get My Free Guide Now" (consistent)

Rule #5: Get Your Audience to Raise Their Hand (Call Out)

Purpose: Immediately tell visitors if this page is for them.

‚ùå Generic:

"Transform Your Business with Our Coaching"


‚úÖ Specific Call Out:

"A Free 10-Minute Guide for Female Founders, Coaches, and Change Makers Whose Fire Feels Dim But Whose Mission Is Far From Done"

Benefits of Clear Call Outs:

  • Attracts ideal customers: "That's me!" reaction
  • Repels wrong customers: They leave quickly (good for your metrics)
  • Improves list quality: Higher engagement, better deliverability
  • Increases conversions: People who stay are pre-qualified

‚úÖ Website Optimization Checklist

1. Readability Check

  • ‚òê Paste website copy into HemingwayApp.com

  • ‚òê Simplify complex words

  • ‚òê Identify your ONE primary call to action
  • ‚òê Remove competing CTAs
  • ‚òê Ensure primary CTA appears 2-3 times on the page
  • ‚òê Use consistent button text
  • ‚òê Make buttons visually prominent

  • ‚òê Create a dedicated landing page for free offer (don't use pop-up)

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistakes to Avoid

‚ùå Mistake #1: Too Many CTAs (Paradox of Choice)

The data: Stores with 5 jam varieties sold MORE than stores with 25 varieties.

Fix: ONE primary CTA per page, repeated 2-3 times

‚ùå Mistake #2: No Dedicated Free Offer Page

The data: +20% email signups, -30% sales (Silva Method)

Fix: Create landing page selling the free offer

‚ùå Mistake #3: Missing Founder Story

Why it hurts: No personal connection, no trust building

Fix: Add 1-minute founder story to About section

‚ùå Mistake #4: No Post-Purchase Onboarding

Why it hurts: They don't use product ‚Üí They don't buy again

Fix: Create Welcome page explaining how to use product
