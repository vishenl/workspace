‚ö° The 5 Golden Rules of Copywriting

Rule #1: Write for No More Than 15-Year-Old Comprehension Level

Why it matters: Multiple studies prove that content matching a 15-year-old's reading level converts better across ALL audiences, regardless of education level.

How to Test:

  1. Go to HemingwayApp.com
  2. Copy your website text
  3. Paste it into Hemingway
  4. Check your grade level (aim for grade 5-8)
‚úÖ Example from Class:

When Henri tested her website, it came back as Grade 5 (approximately 10-11 years old) - Perfect! This proves that even complex transformation work can be explained simply.

Rule #2: Have a Clear Call to Action (CTA)

The Problem: Most websites confuse visitors with multiple competing CTAs.

‚ùå Common Mistake:

"Let's Book Clarity" + "Empower" + "Impact" + "Free Guide" + "Reignite Your Flame"

Result: Visitor confusion = No action taken

‚úÖ Solution:

ONE primary call to action throughout the page:

"Download Your Free Guide"

(appears 2-3 times)

Everything else is secondary or removed.

Rule #3: It's Not About Making a Sale - It's About Getting Users to USE the Product

The Mindvalley Case Study:

Initial Approach:

"Download your free audio and ebook" ‚Üí Pop-up ‚Üí Instant download

Result: 20% MORE emails, but 30% FEWER sales üìâ

Solution:

Create a dedicated landing page explaining what's in the guide, why you need it, benefits you'll receive, and how to use it.
